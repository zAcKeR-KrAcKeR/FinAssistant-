FINSIGHT RISK MANAGEMENT GUIDELINES
Version 2.1 | Effective: March 2024
Classification: Internal Use Only — Risk & Compliance

1. RISK FRAMEWORK OVERVIEW

FinSight operates a three-tier risk management framework aligned with RBI guidelines on credit risk management for Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) and scheduled commercial banks.

The framework covers:
- Credit Risk: Risk of borrower default
- Concentration Risk: Excessive exposure to one segment or geography  
- Operational Risk: Process failures in origination or servicing
- Model Risk: Errors in credit scoring and decisioning models

2. CREDIT RISK TIERS

2.1 Risk Classification Matrix
All active loan accounts are classified into four risk tiers based on a combination of CIBIL score, DTI ratio, credit utilisation, and payment behaviour:

TIER 1 — GREEN (LOW RISK)
Criteria: CIBIL ≥ 720, DTI < 30%, Credit utilisation < 40%, No previous defaults
Expected Default Rate: < 5%
Action: Standard monitoring; quarterly review

TIER 2 — AMBER (MEDIUM RISK)
Criteria: CIBIL 650–719, DTI 30–40%, Credit utilisation 40–65%, ≤ 1 previous default
Expected Default Rate: 5%–12%
Action: Enhanced monitoring; monthly portfolio review; proactive outreach at 15 DPD

TIER 3 — RED (HIGH RISK)
Criteria: CIBIL 550–649, DTI 40–55%, Credit utilisation 65–80%, 1–2 previous defaults
Expected Default Rate: 12%–25%
Action: Intensive monitoring; weekly DPD tracking; restructuring assessment at 30 DPD; field visit at 60 DPD

TIER 4 — BLACK (CRITICAL RISK)
Criteria: CIBIL < 550, DTI > 55%, Credit utilisation > 80%, 2+ previous defaults
Expected Default Rate: > 25%
Action: Immediate escalation to Recovery; legal action assessment at 90 DPD; NPA classification at 90 DPD

3. CONCENTRATION RISK LIMITS

3.1 Geographic Concentration
- No single state should exceed 20% of total portfolio value
- Alert threshold: 15% — triggered for enhanced monitoring
- States currently under watch: Maharashtra (current exposure: 15.2%)
- Maximum district concentration: 5% of portfolio

3.2 Segment Concentration
- No single customer segment should exceed 30% of total portfolio count
- Young Professional segment currently at 28% — monitoring threshold active
- Self-Employed / Freelancer combined exposure capped at 25%

3.3 Product Concentration
- Personal Loans (unsecured): Maximum 35% of portfolio
- Currently at 30% — within limits but approaching threshold

4. EARLY WARNING INDICATORS (EWIs)

The following triggers initiate enhanced monitoring and customer outreach:

4.1 Behavioural Indicators
- Missed EMI (1 instance): SMS + call outreach within 48 hours
- Missed EMI (2 consecutive): Personal contact + repayment plan offer
- High credit card utilisation spike (>80%): Early warning flag
- Multiple new credit enquiries (>3 in 30 days): Stress indicator
- Drop in CIBIL score by >30 points: Alert to Relationship Manager

4.2 Macroeconomic Triggers
- State-level unemployment rate increase >2pp: Portfolio review for affected states
- RBI rate hike >50bps: Stress test on floating rate loans
- Inflation >6% for 3 consecutive months: Income erosion assessment

5. PORTFOLIO STRESS TESTING

5.1 Quarterly Stress Tests
Mandatory stress tests are run quarterly on the full portfolio under three scenarios:

BASELINE: Current economic conditions
- Expected annual default rate: 10–13%
- Required capital buffer: As per regulatory minimum

ADVERSE: Moderate economic stress (GDP growth -1%, unemployment +2pp)
- Expected annual default rate: 16–20%
- Required capital buffer: +25% above baseline

SEVERE: Severe economic downturn (GDP growth -3%, unemployment +5pp)
- Expected annual default rate: 24–30%
- Required capital buffer: +50% above baseline

5.2 Segment-Specific Stress Tests
High-risk segments (Young Professionals, Freelancers, Maharashtra region) undergo monthly stress testing due to elevated baseline risk.

6. NON-PERFORMING ASSET (NPA) MANAGEMENT

6.1 NPA Classification (per RBI guidelines)
- Substandard: 1–12 months overdue
- Doubtful: 12–36 months overdue
- Loss: 36+ months overdue (or unrecoverable per legal assessment)

6.2 Provisioning Requirements
- Standard assets: 0.40% provisioning
- Substandard assets: 15% provisioning
- Doubtful (up to 12 months): 25%
- Doubtful (12–36 months): 40%
- Doubtful (>36 months): 100%
- Loss assets: 100% provisioning

7. MODEL GOVERNANCE

7.1 Credit Scoring Model Review
All credit risk models must be:
- Validated annually by an independent model validation team
- Back-tested against actual default outcomes quarterly
- Reviewed for bias (demographic, geographic) semi-annually
- Documented with assumptions, limitations, and known failure modes

7.2 Model Override Policy
Human underwriters may override model decisions but must document reasons. Override rates are monitored: >15% override rate triggers model recalibration review.

8. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

All risk management practices comply with:
- RBI Master Directions on Credit Risk (2023)
- Basel III capital adequacy norms
- SEBI LODR requirements (for listed entities)
- NBFC-Scale Based Regulation (SBR) Framework
