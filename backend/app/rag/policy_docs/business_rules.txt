FINSIGHT BUSINESS RULES & OPERATIONAL PROCEDURES
Version 1.8 | Effective: January 2024
Classification: Internal Use Only — Operations & Compliance

1. LOAN PROCESSING RULES

1.1 Application Processing TAT (Turnaround Time)
- Personal Loan (salaried, pre-approved): 4 hours
- Personal Loan (standard): 3 business days
- Vehicle Loan: 2 business days
- Home Loan: 10–15 business days
- Business Loan: 7–10 business days
- Education Loan: 5 business days

TAT breach >20% in any month triggers process review.

1.2 Mandatory Checks
ALL loan applications must undergo the following before approval:
a) CIBIL bureau check (mandatory; not waivable)
b) CIBIL Equifax cross-check (for loans >INR 10L)
c) Anti-money laundering (AML) screening
d) PEP (Politically Exposed Person) check
e) CKYC (Central KYC) verification
f) Income verification (bank statement analysis)
g) Employment/business verification (for loans >INR 5L)

1.3 Duplicate Application Check
System automatically flags if same applicant (PAN-based) has:
- Active application at another branch: Hold for 48 hours, verify intent
- Application rejected in last 6 months: Require Senior UW approval
- Active loan with 30+ DPD: Automatic decline; no exceptions

2. LOAN FLAGGING RULES

2.1 Automatic Flag Triggers
A loan application is automatically flagged for enhanced review if ANY of the following apply:
- CIBIL score < 620
- DTI ratio > 45%
- Previous defaults ≥ 2
- Credit utilisation > 75% on existing cards
- Income-to-loan ratio < 0.15 (annual income less than 15% of loan amount)
- Age of borrower > 65 at loan maturity
- Application from a state with >13% default rate in last 12 months
- Self-employed with <3 years business vintage
- Loan purpose: Speculative investment (stocks, crypto)

2.2 Fraud Indicator Rules
Immediate escalation to Fraud Control Unit if:
- Address mismatch between application and KYC documents
- Employer phone number does not match corporate directory
- Income claimed significantly higher than industry norms for stated job role
- Multiple applications with identical income/employment details (possible identity fraud)
- Bank statement shows large cash deposits preceding application (possible income manipulation)

3. INTEREST RATE RULES

3.1 Base Rate Determination
Interest rates are set monthly by the Asset-Liability Committee (ALCO) based on:
- RBI repo rate + spread
- Cost of funds
- Credit risk premium
- Market competitive positioning

3.2 Customer-Level Rate Setting
Final rate = Base Rate + Risk Premium (per CIBIL tier) + Product Premium + Negotiation Discount
Maximum negotiation discount: 0.50% (for CIBIL >750, relationship customers)
Minimum rate floor: No loan below MCLR (marginal cost of lending rate)

4. LOAN RESTRUCTURING RULES

4.1 Eligibility for Restructuring
A loan may be restructured if:
- Borrower demonstrates genuine financial hardship (job loss, medical emergency, business disruption)
- Account is Standard or Substandard (not Doubtful or Loss)
- Borrower has made at least 12 EMI payments
- No previous restructuring in the last 36 months

4.2 Restructuring Options
a) EMI holiday: Maximum 3 months; interest continues to accrue
b) Repayment extension: Maximum 24 months additional tenure
c) Interest rate reduction: Maximum 1.50% reduction; requires Credit Manager approval
d) Partial settlement: Subject to Board approval; minimum 80% principal recovery

5. COLLECTIONS RULES

5.1 Collection Escalation Matrix
- 0–7 DPD: Automated SMS and email reminders
- 8–15 DPD: Outbound call from collections team; offer repayment plan
- 16–30 DPD: Senior collections officer; home visit for loans >INR 5L
- 31–60 DPD: Restructuring assessment; legal notice served for secured loans
- 61–90 DPD: Recovery team engagement; field visit mandatory
- 90+ DPD: NPA classification; legal proceedings initiated

5.2 Customer Communication Standards
All customer communication must:
- Comply with RBI Fair Practices Code
- Not be made between 8 PM and 8 AM
- Not involve threats, intimidation, or public shaming
- Be conducted in language preferred by customer
- Be documented in CRM system within 24 hours

6. DATA AND REPORTING RULES

6.1 Bureau Reporting
- All accounts must be reported to CIBIL, Equifax, and Experian monthly
- Delinquency status updated within 45 days of each period end
- Corrections to bureau data must be processed within 30 days of customer dispute

6.2 Regulatory Reporting
- Monthly: Portfolio MIS to Board Risk Committee
- Quarterly: NPA and provision report to RBI
- Semi-annual: Credit risk model performance report
- Annual: Full portfolio audit by external rating agency

6.3 Data Retention
Customer data retained for 8 years post loan closure per RBI data localisation norms.
Sensitive data (KYC, bank statements) encrypted at rest using AES-256.

7. VOICE ASSISTANT & AI SYSTEM RULES

7.1 AI-Assisted Decision Making
The FinSight AI assistant is classified as a Decision Support System (DSS), not an autonomous decision system. All AI-generated recommendations require human review before implementation.

7.2 AI Query Handling Rules
- The system may answer portfolio-level analytical queries
- Individual customer PII (name, address, phone) must not be returned in AI responses
- Risk scores generated by AI models must carry uncertainty bands
- AI responses must indicate data source and confidence level

7.3 Voice Interface Guidelines
- Voice queries are processed with consent; recordings not stored beyond session
- Transcription may be used for quality improvement with customer consent
- Voice responses use synthesised speech; no cloning of human voices

8. COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT

8.1 Policy Compliance
Deviation from any rule in this document requires:
- Written justification
- Approval from authority one level above normal
- Documentation in exception register (retained 3 years)

8.2 Audit Rights
Internal Audit has unrestricted access to all loan files, system logs, and decision records.
Annual surprise audits conducted on minimum 5% of portfolio.
